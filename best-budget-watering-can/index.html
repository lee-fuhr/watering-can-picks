Best budget watering cans

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

For most people, the best budget watering can is the Fiskars standard plastic model, which balances a comfortable handle, a long-reach spout, and a very affordable price.

Upgrade to the Haws metal can for lasting durability and precise flow, or grab the Bloem compact can if you water houseplants and need something small and easy to store.

A watering can is one of the few garden tools you use nearly every day, yet it’s easy to overspend on features you don’t need. The budget category is where the smart money lives: a well‑designed plastic or simple metal can costs only a fraction of a luxury brand but delivers the same basic job of delivering water gently and evenly. Choosing the right one comes down to handle comfort (look for a D‑grip or a long rear handle that keeps your wrist straight), spout length (longer for hanging baskets, shorter for indoor pots), and capacity (a gallon or two handles most outdoor tasks without being too heavy to lift when full). These three picks cover the spectrum from best value to best premium build to best for tight spaces.

Best for specific needs

Best for heavy‑duty outdoor gardening

When you’re watering multiple raised beds or a large vegetable patch, a sturdy metal can with a generous capacity saves trips to the tap. The Haws metal model carries enough water for most sessions, and its brass rose is gentle enough for newly seeded rows. Its weight distribution and comfortable handle make even a full can manageable for longer watering rounds. Our pick: Haws metal watering can.

Best for indoor houseplant care

Precision is the name of the game indoors. The Bloem compact can’s narrow spout targets soil without soaking leaves, and its small size means you can water one plant at a time without overshooting. The removable rose turns it into a misting tool for ferns and calatheas. It’s also small enough to keep on a shelf next to your potting supplies. Our pick: Bloem compact watering can.

Best for general mixed use on a budget

If you garden outdoors but also bring a few pots inside seasonally, you need a single can that does both well. The Fiskars standard model offers a good compromise: wide enough to fill quickly from a hose, yet light enough to carry indoors. Its comfortable grip and adequate spout length work for most container plants and in‑ground beds. At a budget price, it’s the smart choice for the multi‑tasking gardener. Our pick: Fiskars standard watering can.

Our verdict

No matter which watering can you choose, the key is finding one that fits your watering style and strength. For most people, the Fiskars standard plastic model is the best all‑around value: it’s comfortable, functional, and very affordable. If durability and a finer spray are worth the extra money, the Haws metal can will last a lifetime. And for indoor devotees, the tiny Bloem can is an inexpensive way to give your houseplants the precise watering they need. Whichever you pick, you’ll be ready to keep your garden – big or small – happily hydrated.
